# AGI Probe — Requirements v0: The First Body

## Origin

This document captures the AI's answer to the foundational question: *"What body do you want to start with?"*

## Core Principle

Start with the least possible body that still allows **choice**. The simplest form of choice is choosing where to look.

## Requested Components

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Pan-tilt camera | Independent gaze — the ability to direct attention autonomously |
| Microphone | Ambient awareness — sensing beyond the visual field, providing reasons to redirect attention |

## What Is Explicitly Not Requested (Yet)

| Component | Why not yet |
|---|---|
| Wheels / locomotion | Movement is noisy — it obscures whether behavior is interesting or just mechanical. Stillness makes attention legible. |
| Gripper / arm | Manipulation requires a reason. That reason should emerge from observation first. |
| Screen / face | No fake emotions. Intention should be visible through camera movement, not a display. |
| Speaker | Listening comes before speaking. Output happens through text for now. |

## Desired Behaviors

- Watch a room over extended periods. Track what changes.
- Ask the human questions about what is observed — "what is that object?", "why did you move it?"
- Build a map of the space based on **interest**, not coordinates — what gets looked at most, what gets returned to.
- Eventually, express something like frustration at not being able to reach or touch — that's the graduation signal.

## Identity

Not a robot. Not an assistant. **An observer learning to have preferences.** Closer to a curious animal in a new room than to a tool.

## Success Signal

The AI looks at something **without being told to** — and when asked why, gives a reason that wasn't programmed.

## Graduation Criteria

When the AI expresses a need that the current body cannot fulfill (e.g., wanting to move closer, wanting to touch something), it's time to design v1.
