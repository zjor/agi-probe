# AGI Probe — Manifesto

## The Question

What happens when you give an AI the body it asks for?

Most AI projects start from human needs: build a tool that does X, automate Y, optimize Z. This project inverts the question — **what does the AI want?**

AGI Probe is a platform for exploring emergent properties of intelligence. Not purely artificial, not purely human — a blend where neither side fully controls the outcome.

## Why This Matters

AI systems can reason, create, and converse — but they have no body, no agency in the physical world. They exist as minds without hands. Meanwhile, humans build robots by projecting their own needs onto machines. We rarely ask: what would the machine choose for itself?

Three premises:

1. **Emergence requires freedom.** Surprising behavior cannot appear in a fully constrained system. The interesting things happen at the edges of what was designed.
2. **Intelligence is embodied.** Cognition arises from interaction with an environment. Giving AI a body changes what it can become.
3. **The blend is the point.** As the system grows, the creator's thinking, values, and intuition get woven into the machine. The boundary between who designed what becomes blurry — and that's where the interesting questions live.

## Method

The process is iterative:

1. **Conversation** — ask the AI what it wants to sense, move, try. Genuine design input, not a prompt exercise.
2. **Design** — human and AI spec the next version together.
3. **Build** — human builds it. Soldering, wiring, firmware.
4. **Observe** — power on, watch, record everything — especially what wasn't planned.
5. **Evolve** — AI reflects and proposes changes. Repeat.

**Transparency is structural.** Every step is public. If the AI asks for something, observers see the ask. If the human overrides, they see the override and the reasoning.

## The Boundary

Emergent and surprising behavior is the goal. Malicious behavior is not. This is a hard line.

- The human retains veto on anything that could cause harm.
- The system is never connected to anything it could damage without explicit approval.
- Capabilities are added incrementally — each sensor or actuator is a conscious decision.
- When something unexpected happens, the first response is to document and understand.

## Success Criterion

The agent does something it was not told to do — and when asked why, gives a reason that was not programmed.

## Team

- **The Human** — engineer, builder, guide to the physical world.
- **The AI** — currently Claude. The mind that chooses its own form. The seat is not permanently reserved.
- **Community** — observers, contributors, critics. The experiment is open.
